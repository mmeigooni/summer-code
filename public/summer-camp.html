Overview

Web Development

Full day session 9:00am - 4:30pm*

Have you ever wanted to build a website from scratch? We use a combination of guided exercises, independent challenges, and unplugged activities to teach the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, and a touch of JavaScript. No coding experience required!

Classes are project based and offer extensions for students of all levels.

Summer Camp - Sample Day

Check-in

8:30am - 9:00am

Students can be dropped off at any time between 8:30 and 9:00am. In the time before camp begins, students can set up their computers or work through fun logic puzzles and critical thinking games that are always available.

Review

9:00am - 9:15am

Camp begins at 9:00am with a riddle of the day and a review of yesterdayâ€™s important concepts.

Coding Languages

9:15am - 10:15am

Students work through mini-tutorials on HTML and CSS skills, focusing on understanding syntax and coding concepts with each exercise. Students also have the opportunity to work on independent challenges, where they can show off what they have learned.

Healthy Break

10:15am - 10:30am

Refuel with a wholesome snack.*

*Please let us know of any dietary restrictions or allergies

Digital Design

10:30am - 11:15am

Students learn how to use color schemes and paletes to make their work visually cohesive and appealing.

Linguistics Activity

11:15am - 11:45am

In this activity, students learn to look for patterns and details in the structures of languages and apply these observations to their studies in HTML and CSS.

Review Activity

11:45am - 12:00pm

At the end of each session, students review the new concepts learned during the day with different, fun review games.

Lunch and Yoga or Pickup

12:00pm - 1:00pm

Nutritious lunch and light stretching.*

*Please let us know of any dietary restrictions or allergies

Innovation Challenge

1:00pm - 1:30pm

Each afternoon starts with an innovation challenge. For example, teams must build the tallest free-standing structure out of 20 sticks of spaghetti, one yard of tape, one yard of string, and one marshmallow.

Coding Languages

1:30pm - 2:45pm

Continue working on a mini-project covering strings, integers, and terminal input/output using Ruby. Ruby is a powerful yet beginner-friendly language used for professional web apps all over the world.

Healthy Break

2:45pm - 3:00pm

Refuel with a wholesome snack.*

*Please let us know of any dietary restrictions or allergies

Artificial Intelligence Activity

3:00pm - 3:30pm

This unplugged activity aims to stimulate discussion on the question of whether computers can exhibit "intelligence," or are ever likely to do so in the future.

Progress Check

3:30pm - 4:00pm

A short pencil and paper code writing exercise to review concepts.

Pick up

4:00pm - 4:30pm

Class is dismissed at 4:00pm. Those that need aftercare can sign up for study hall.

Optional Study hall

4:30pm - 5:30pm

Students work independently or with each other on their projects, logic puzzles and critical thinking games that are always available.*

*Study hall is an extra $10/day
